Snapchat

Snapchat is one of the biggest and most widely used social media apps in the world and is open and easy to use for all age ranges. Due to this, Snapchat would be perfect for educational use. In the app, you send pictures called ‘Snaps’ to friends which usually also features texts in the pictures. You can also send video and even post them to your ‘Story’ which shows you pictures or videos to all your friends. It is also easy to sign up for and the interface is simple for your average user.

Registering for the App

Step 1: When you hit sign-up, you will need to enter your name.

Step 2: It will ask for your birthday. You need to be 13+ years old to use the app.

Step 3: Once you've entered your age, you'll need to enter a username.

Step 4: After, you'll need to create a password. It needs to be 8+ characters long.

Step 5: After creating your password, you will need to enter your email.

Step 6: You will then need to enter your mobile number after entering your email.

Cautions

When it comes to social media in general, some basic things should be known when setting up an account and actually using them: Never share your password to anyone, even if your account is private, it could still reach a wider audience and think about what you post. On Snapchat, only the people you add can view your story. You can also post public stories which can be dangerous as anyone in the world could view them. Be careful who you add as they could send you snaps. If you wanna be extra careful, you can post private stories for close friends.

Educational Uses:

  • As a teacher, you could use the app to post any bits of work on your story or directly send any work to a student who may have missed out on a class.
  • As a student, you can snap your teacher or fellow students any questions very easily and quickly.
  • It is useful overall for mostly students to share stuff about class, homework, or even help out eachother.
